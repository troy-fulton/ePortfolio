Lockheed Martin Enterprise Operations

In summer 2018, I worked in Grand Prairie, Texas for Lockheed Martin's Enterprise Operations as an Applications Intern. As a part of the Data Analytics Team, I aided in providing internal customers with applications to improve business processes across multiple business functions, such as production operations. Throughout the summer, I was given professional advice on how to advance my career from leaders in the industry on topics such as how to network and improve my chances of landing a leadership role.

Although I did not return to Lockheed the next summer, I found that my experience developing software were extremely handy in many of my future endeavors. My projects gave me a sense of what it was like to carry out team software development using tools like git, and they showed me how to connect with the customer or customers' needs to deliver the best application possible. I am proud of the work I completed at Lockheed Martin because it showed that I am capable of growing as a software developer if I put in the effort to be constantly learning new tools. I learned the gratification of receiving positive feedback and the iterative process that occurs when I do not. The lessons I learned from my experiences will stay with me for the rest of the career, as has already been evident: I already apply my learning about team software to parts of both my personal and professional life, as I have in my team projects in and out of class and have been in development of personal projects.

At the end of my internship, I presented my two projects to the VP of Enterprise Applications. For the first part of the summer, I learned Python well enough to program a web scraper using the lxml and the requests library to gain competitive intelligence. From this project, I learned the importance of data integrity, since I needed to take the unstructured data of several html documents and convert them to json files. I demonstrated to the Vice President that in order to preserve the integrity of our data, we needed to either vigorously code to handle many strange situations or continue updating our algorithm for extracting data.

For my second project, I trained to use the SAP HANA database to help internal Accounting and Production Operations employees more safely, quickly, and easily access their data to make small daily changes. We crafted a JavaScript application using SAP HANA XS (Extended Services) to carefully display requested data to the customers and allow them to edit data one piece at a time. Because of this application, we were able to improve an otherwise costly business process. Now that individuals who have access but not necessarily training to use tools such as SAP HANA can successfully navigate their data without the overhead of the Database Management System.

The main technical skills I took away were my knowledge of Python, SQL, JavaScript, basic network ideologies (which helped me in my then future Networks class), some basic forecasting concepts in R, and Tableau. I also walked away with a better idea of how to work well in a company so dependent on data and software, how to practice good software engineering techniques, and above all, an overall bolstered self-confidence in my technical problem-solving abilities.

About Me

My name is Troy Fulton, and I am a Computer Science Major from The Woodlands, Texas, but more importantly, I am the loudest proudest member of the Fightin' Texas Aggie Class of 2020. Whoop!




I was born and raised in The Woodlands, Texas, and I lived there in an Aggie family until I moved to College Station to start at Texas A&M University in August 2016. I love traveling, solving puzzles, and exploring the great outdoors. I have been playing trumpet since I was 10 and love music so much I almost decided to perform professionally after high school. I enjoy spending time with friends and am constantly searching for ways to challenge myself.



Ever since I fell in love with programming during my freshman year, I have learned to approach everything with an open mind. The more I study computer science, the more I realize I don't know about the world and how much better I learn when I admit that I don't and can't know everything. I was not entirely sure what I wanted to do with my Aerospace Engineering Degree after I graduated, but I was ready to soak up as much knowledge as I could to help guide my decision. I spent my first year exploring my interest with an open mind and found that my passion resides in computers, and I'm so glad I did.

Texas A&M University has been an excellent fit for me. When I first started here, I lived on campus in McFadden Hall as part of the University Honors Program. I quickly made friends with a community of scholars across a myriad of disciplines whom I may not have gotten the chance to learn from otherwise. Being a part of the University Honors Program has not only enriched my education in many ways, but also opened many doors to new academic and professional opportunities. I enjoy playing trumpet with the Wind Symphony here, as it has given me an avenue to express my appreciation for music, balance the hemispheres of my brain, and make new friends with others who share my passion for making music. Texas A&M University is a place where curiosity thrives. I am lucky that as long as I remain passionate about learning as much as I can, fellow Aggies will always support my educational efforts.



I am striving to complete the Texas A&M University Undergraduate Outcomes as I set out to fulfill the requirements of my degree. I am constantly working to gain depth and breadth in my coursework by taking classes that challenge me to think critically about how I can master the knowledge and apply my knowledge as a professional. Experiences like my internships and research give me the chance to develop my communication skills. I work to practice social and personal responsibility by involving myself in my local community and by working to become a more global citizen. I aim to involve myself in collaborative activities by engaging in various activities, such as those I do through school that prepare me for lifelong learning.



As of now, I am interning at Texas Instruments for the summer, and I have a lot planned before I graduate! In August, I will be heading to Peru to study Engineering Ethics, and beginning Fall 2019, I will be preparing my thesis in interconnection networks. I have completed much of my computer science education, so now, I am focusing on applying what I have learned to the DSP processors at TI. I am hopeful that I will get a return offer and continue writing software to improve TI's line of products and contribute to their global footprint.

Texas Instruments

For much of summer 2019, I will be staying in Sugar Land, Texas to work as a Software Engineering Intern with Texas Instruments on the compiler team in SDTO. I am currently working on a project to improve performance on a DSP device software, so I am learning a great deal about software pipelining, SIMD (Single Instruction Multiple Data) and vectorization, and how compilers work.

In Spring 2018, I enjoyed learning about computer architecture so much that I decided to embark on Texas A&M University's Embedded Systems Integration Minor in Fall 2018 with ESET classes. Since Texas Instruments has such close ties with Texas A&M University, I was able to quickly start learning how to use the hardware and software capabilities of a new architecture, which prepared me for the learning curve at TI. I consider myself lucky to get to work at the home of the DSP, and I enjoy learning about the advances in embedded processing and analog technology in its past, present, and future.

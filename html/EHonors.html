ACE Scholars (Engineering Honors- CS Track)

I joined my cohort of Engineering Honors in August 2018, meaning that the honors activities I participated in prior to then were a part of University Honors. See the University Honors page for more details.

Honors Activities

As an Engineering Honors student, I am also required to attend two events each semester. These events, however, are usually geared towards either learning more about research in our department or landing a top notch career. For example, I usually attend the EH Course Scheduling Social, where I counsel freshmen and other honors students about how to most successfully plan their careers.

Another event I like to attend is the Spring Banquet. At the spring banquet, awards are given to students and faculty who have excelled in their work or committment to Aggie education. Since I received a scholarship, I was invited to attend and meet my donor.

Honors Courses

As mentioned on the University Honors page, one of the major advantages Honors is the ability to register early for honors courses. I like to register for these classes because I get a better insight into whatever topic I'm exploring, and with such a small class size, the professor can more easily focus on the needs of the class and give students more individual attention. Small class sizes of motivated, excelling students also provides a more collaborative atmosphere of learning. To learn more about specific honors classes I've taken, see Coursework.

Research

Engineering Honors requires that I choose a professor to perform research under. During my senior year, I studied On-Chip Networks and Memory Networks under Dr. E.J. Kim, my Computer Organization professor (see here).

Scholarship

For the 2018-2019 academic year, I was honored to be awarded a $1000 scholarship from the Industrial Affiliates Program. The IAP has provided many benefits to Engineering Honors students, and I am thankful that they considered me among my ACE Scholar peers to be awarded this scholarship.

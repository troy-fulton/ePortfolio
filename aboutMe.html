About Me

I was born and raised in the Conroe/ Woodlands area of Texas where I lived until I moved to College Station to start at
Texas A&M University in August 2016. I was not entirely sure what I wanted to do with my Aerospace Engineering
Degree after I graduated, but I was ready to soak up as much knowledge as I could to help guide my decision. Now
that I have finished a year in the Computer Science Department, I am convinced that I was meant to be a developer.

Texas A&M University has been an excellent fit for me. I enjoy playing the trumpet with the Wind Symphony on campus, as it
has given me an avenue to both express my appreciation for music and make new friends with others who share
that appreciation. I have also made many friends in other disciplines through the University Honors program that I may not
have meet otherwise. The University Honors Program has not only enriched my education
in many ways, but also opened many doors to new academic and professional opportunities.

I am striving to complete the Texas A&M Undergraduate Outcomes as I set out to fulfill the requirements of my degree.
As I approach the final class of my Mathematics Minor and begin the first class of my new Embedded Systems Integration
Minor, I am also working hard to complete the goals set out by the University Honors and Engineering Honors Program. I
look forward to completing my senior capstone project that I will hopefully complete through the Undergraduate Research
Scolars Program before I graduate in May 2020.

I am constantly looking for ways to take what I have learned about development from my various learning experiences and apply
them to real-world problems. I have learned a great deal about software development methods in my Programming Studio course (Fall
2018), such as test-driven development and SCRUM, and I am actively seeking opportunities to practice new methodologies alongside
other professionals as soon as summer 2019. Now that I have finished my internship with Lockheed Martin, I believe that I have
dramatically improved my technical maturity and competence as well as my business maturity. I believe the next step in my education
is to perform research alongisde a professor so that I can have a much deeper understanding of my field and contribute to the
field more than the average person.
